Crane Operator — Precision at Scale

Moving tonnes with millimetre accuracy, reading wind, managing blind lifts — that spatial judgement is rare. Now you can get paid for it.

The Observer Programme pays experienced crane operators to record their working day using lightweight smart glasses and a wristband. You do your normal lifts — we capture the expertise.

How it works

  • Wear smart glasses and a wristband during your shift
  • Record sessions from 30 minutes up — fits around your lifts
  • Spend a few minutes after each session confirming what tasks you performed
  • Get paid per recorded minute. The more you record, the more you earn
  • All recordings are anonymised — no faces, no voices, no identifying details

What you'll earn

Crane operation is a high-scarcity specialism and earns premium rates.

€55–€85 per recorded hour

€220–€340 per recording day (based on ~4 hours)

€3,200–€4,500 per month (based on 20 recording days)

First Observers in your specialism or region earn a launch bonus. Extra earnings possible through referral bonuses.

What we're looking for

  • 3+ years of crane operating experience
  • Currently working — employed or freelance
  • Active crane operation: lifting, slewing, load management
  • Valid certifications/tickets for your crane type
  • Smartphone (iOS or Android)

All specialisms welcome: tower crane operator, mobile crane operator, overhead gantry crane operator, telehandler operator.

We provide everything

  • Smart glasses — shipped to you, pre-configured
  • Full onboarding and support (~30 minutes)
  • Dedicated contact person
  • Biometric wristband if you don't own a compatible fitness tracker

About the Observer Programme

The Observer Programme by Aisthis works with professionals across 100+ trades to document how skilled work actually happens. Crane operation — the spatial awareness, the load judgement, the environmental reading — is some of the most valuable specialist knowledge on any site.

Applications are reviewed with the help of AI. We'll be in touch if there's a match.
